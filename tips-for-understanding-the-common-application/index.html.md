# Navigating the Common App: 7 Essential Tips for Stress\-Free College Applications

For some students, it’s nearly impossible to understand the Common Application! The Common Application, or Common App as you might often hear it called, is an online application widely used by American colleges and some European universities. It’s important to understand the Common Application fully before you begin applying through it, so read these tips on how to do just that!

Navigating the college admissions process can feel like a full-time job. Whether you're looking for [fun colleges with great reputations](https://lifestyle.allwomenstalk.com/fun-colleges-with-the-best-reputations/) or focusing on elite institutions, getting your application right is the first step. Here is a quick breakdown of what to expect:

| Feature | What You Need | Why It Matters |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Personal Info | Basic demographics | Identification |
| Academic History | Transcripts & GPA | Admissions criteria |
| Extracurriculars | Clubs, sports, work | Shows personality |
| Personal Essay | Your unique story | Sets you apart |

## 1. Sit Down with Your Parents

Even if your parents didn’t attend college, they can help you understand the Common Application. Having two sets of eyes is always better than having one, especially when it comes to something as important as your future. Plus, there is a section on the Common App that requires parent information, and there may be other questions you don’t understand. So before you set out to tackle this application, make sure you have your mom or dad by your side for support.

## 2. Make a Resume

The Common App requires a lot of information, which can be hard to summon up all at once. To make things easier on yourself, write an academic resume before you begin applying to colleges and universities. An academic resume can help organize your thoughts and help you figure out how to make yourself sound the most impressive. Once your resume is complete, simply transfer the information onto your Common App! You might also find it helpful to look into [why teenagers should work](https://lifestyle.allwomenstalk.com/important-reasons-why-teenagers-should-work/) to bolster your resume experience.

## 3. Follow the Links

The Common App doesn’t leave you to wonder what you’re supposed to do all on your own. The makers of the application understand that this is a confusing process for many, so they provide links to instructions given in greater detail. When filling out your Common Application, make sure you click on the links around the margin of the web page. Follow them to find the answers to frequently asked questions about the application process on the [official Common App website](https://www.commonapp.org/apply/first-year-students).

## 4. Visit Your College's Website

The colleges you want to attend will have loads of information on applying! Look for their pages designed for freshman applicants, and you'll find checklists for all you need to do on the Common App website and beyond to get yourself accepted. For example, check out the admissions pages for top schools like [Harvard University](https://college.harvard.edu/admissions) or [Stanford University](https://admissions.stanford.edu/) to see their specific requirements.

## 5. Take Your Time

It's infinitely important that you take your time filling out the Common App! Rushing the process can lead to mistakes that are easily avoidable when you are diligent. When you rush filling out the Common Application, it’s easy to skip over steps you may not notice or totally understand. Speeding through your application is a regrettable decision, so slow and steady really does win the race. Focus on quality over speed to ensure your [entry into a good college](https://lifestyle.allwomenstalk.com/reasons-it-is-so-important-to-get-into-a-good-college/) is smooth.

## 6. Triple Check Your Application

Along with taking your time, it’s important to review your application in order to avoid silly mistakes. Double and triple-checking your application can’t hurt, and the more you go over it, the better you will understand it. So before you rush to hit submit and release this weight from your chest, make sure you go over your application as many times as possible. Ensure all your data matches your official records exactly.

## 7. Talk to Your Counselor

It’s your counselor’s job to help you understand everything college-related. If you're bursting with questions and the internet isn't helping, schedule an appointment with your school counselor. They will be happy to help you make sense of this process and can offer professional guidance tailored to your specific situation.

Checklist for a smooth application:

- Confirm all deadlines for each school
- Request letters of recommendation early
- Proofread your personal essay multiple times
- Verify that your test scores have been sent

Overall, understanding the Common Application won’t be easy on your own. But if you seek help from trusted adults, use all of your resources, and take your time going over the application, you should be good to go! What confuses you most about the Common App? How do you manage to understand it?
