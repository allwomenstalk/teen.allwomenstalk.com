# 7 Very Useful Tips on How to Feel at Home at a New School ...

Read on to discover a few very helpful tips on how to feel at home at a new school. Starting a new school can be a pretty challenging experience, but change is a big part of life and you’ll need to learn how to adapt in order to face and overcome all the obstacles that might appear in your path. The first day at a new school can be filled with excitement but at the same time, it can also be a nerve-wracking experience, both for you and your parents. With a bit of patience, you will learn how to benefit from this experience and how to feel at home at a new school. Here is what you should do:

## 1. Reassure Yourself

One of the most important things you can do if you want to learn how to feel at home at a new school is to constantly reassure yourself. Just tell yourself that you will make new friends, that you will feel comfortable and that you will fit right in. Even if you think that you’ll be the weirdest kid in your class, this just isn’t true. I’m sure there are a lot of kids who share your interests and who will be more than happy to be your friends.

## 2. Smile

Always remember to smile, even if you don’t really feel like it. You will seem more open, more approachable and friendlier. Who doesn’t like someone who smiles all the time? So, walk standing straight, keep your chin up and put a big smile on your face. You will see that shortly your mood will improve and you will feel better in no time.

## 3. Be Yourself

Even if you are a bit scared and you worry that people might not like you, try to be yourself and don’t hide behind a mask. You are a wonderful person, you have a lot of friends at your old school and I’m sure you’ll be able to make a lot of new friends at your new school too. Your old classmates appreciate your qualities and love you for the wonderful person that you are. Don’t try to be someone else, someone you’re not! Be genuine and simply be yourself!

## 4. Keep in Touch with Your Old Friends

Just because you moved and now you’re going to a new school, it doesn’t mean that you have to abandon your old friends. Just keep in touch, share your new experiences with them and ask for their advice. They will surely offer you the support you need to feel more at home at your new school and they will be there for you when you will need them!

## 5. Don’t Be Afraid to Make New Friends!

Even if you’re going to a new school and you’re feeling a bit scared or homesick, don’t be afraid to make new friends! Just go up to people and introduce yourself! Smile, ask questions and try to get to know them better. I’m sure you’ll discover that you have similar interests and that it’s actually not that hard to make new friends.

## 6. Do Things You and Your Old Friends Used to do

I’m sure there are a lot of things you and your old friends used to do, things you enjoyed and that you still like doing. Just because you moved to a new school, that doesn’t mean you have to give them up. Maybe you took pictures together, or maybe you used to read all kinds of magazines together or you simply chatted about random stuff. It doesn’t matter what you used to do, the important thing is to keep on doing it, even if you’re at a new school.

## 7. Stay Positive!

Try to build optimism and always stay positive, even if you feel a little scared or lonely sometimes. Who doesn’t like a positive and optimistic person? I’m sure you will find yourself surrounded with a lot of friends in no time and you will even enjoy your new school. Try to focus on the wonderful things in your life and be grateful for everything that you have!

Going to a new school can sometimes be a pretty stressful experience. Just have patience and try to make new friends and you will shortly feel more welcomed and more at home at your new school.
