Ultimate List of Tips to Help You Revise Smarter ...

Exercise • Take Breaks • Take Care • Meditate • Record Yourself • More ...

By Neecey  · Oct 28, 2025

Knowing you have to revise can make you feel like you have to be attached at the hip to your books and notes. If you can't get into the right mindset, don't have the right tools or plan, you can find yourself flailing around, and ultimately failing. What you need before you even think about staring is this list of best revision tips ... ever ... like ever, ever,

1. Exercise

The healthier your body is, the healthier your mind is and the more enthused you will be to be productive and revise.

2. Take Breaks

Don’t power through. Take a ten-minute break every hour to recharge your batteries.

3. Take Care

Don’t put revision above your own health; you will be no good during exams if you are ill.

4. Meditate

Take some time out to meditate. Focusing your mind can do wonders for boosting productivity.

5. Record Yourself

Make voice notes of important facts to learn so that you can revise while you are on the move.

6. Apps

Make the most of all the brilliant revision apps that are available for tablets and smartphones nowadays.

7. Sync Notes

Sync your notes between your phone, tablet and computer so that you can always get to them in any situation.

8. Sleep

It’s vital that you get the regular, recommended amount of sleep per night; your brain needs time to relax and refresh.

9. Less Caffeine

Don’t overdo it with energy drinks and coffee; it will have the opposite affect that you are hoping for.

10. Daylight

If you can, revise outside; the sunlight has wonderful powers for productivity and enthusiasm.

11. Avoid Late Night Snacks

You can get in to a better sleeping regime ready to revise again tomorrow by not having any late night snacks.

12. Exam Calendar

Mark down all the important dates of exams so that you can plan your revision accordingly.

13. Past Papers

Find some past papers to look through and get a feel of the kinds of questions; they can be helpful in guiding your revision.

14. Study Group

If you work better in a team, organize a study group with some friends to help each other prepare.

15. Teach Friends

Sometimes details stick in your head better if you are telling them to somebody else. Test it out by teaching a friend your revision.

16. Repeat Notes

Help your revision to cement itself in your mind by writing out your notes for second or even third time. Repetition is key.

17. Flashcards

Flashcards should be your new best friend. You can take them anywhere and the bite size information really sticks in your brain.

18. Make a Video

Make a video of yourself saying some key revision facts that you can turn to when you can’t bear to read anymore.

19. Word Clouds

Make as many world clouds as you can. They are a great way to encourage your brain to expand on a single idea.

20. Mind Maps

Mind maps perform exactly the same function as word clouds but for a wider topic.

21. Use Colors

The brain responds better to an array of different colours, so use more than just a blue or black pen.

22. Easy Font

Make sure you type your notes out in an easy to read, simple looking font. You don’t want any distractions.

23. No Block Text

Spread your notes out in nice, easy to read lines. Avoid big blocks of text that are going to switch your brain off.

24. Highlighters

Use highlighter pens to pick out the most important facts that you need from your revision.

25. Say It Aloud

Talk to yourself while you are revising. Verbalizing details often makes them stick better.

26. Sing It

Even better, turn it in to a song if you can. This is especially effective for foreign language revision.

27. Different Accent

Make sure your brain is switched on by saying your revision out loud in a different accent!

28. No Booze

Stay away from alcohol when you need to revise. Even a single glass of wine can reduce memory by ten percent.

29. Fish

Have fish for dinner, the omega-3 content will help to boost your brain activity.

24. Stay Hydrated

Our brains are at the most healthy when we are fully hydrated; remember to drink plenty of water.

25. Good Diet

Try to maintain a good diet when you are revising. Junk food will make you feel sluggish.

26. Timetable

Take some initiative and make a timetable for your studies so you always know what you should be doing.

27. Allow Time

Be realistic and begin your revision early enough to get everything done before the deadline/exam.

28. Folders

Keep revision for all your different subjects nice and separate with the help of a few trusty organizing folders.

29. Library

Get away from the distractions of home by going and revising in your nearest library. Silence is golden!

30. Tidy

Tidy up your revision space before your begin. A clean room equals a clean mind.

31. Classical Music

It has been proven that having classical music on in the background can boost your productivity levels.

32. Lighting

Make sure that the lighting in your room is strong enough, as if it is too low you will get an early headache.

33. Daily Targets

Set yourself daily targets for how much revision you want to get done, and stick to them.

34. Be Realistic

Don’t go in expecting to learn and entire subject in a day. Be realistic with your expectations.

35. Treat Yourself

Be sure to reward yourself when you have had a productive session. It will make you feel so good!

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I make my study sessions more effective?
Break your study time into focused chunks with short breaks to keep your mind fresh and attentive.
What's the best way to organize my revision materials?
Use color-coded notes or digital apps to categorize topics for easier recall and review.
How do I stay motivated while revising?
Set small, achievable goals and reward yourself after completing each to keep motivation high.
Should I study alone or with friends?
Both work! Solo study helps focus, while group sessions can clarify doubts and make learning fun.
How can I improve my memory for exams?
Try active recall by testing yourself and use mnemonic devices to remember key information.
Is it helpful to use past exam papers?
Yes, practicing past papers familiarizes you with the exam format and highlights important topics.
How much sleep do I need during revision time?
Aim for at least 7-8 hours a night to keep your brain sharp and reduce stress.
What should I eat to boost concentration?
Choose brain-friendly snacks like nuts, fruits, and whole grains to keep energy steady.
How do I avoid procrastination when revising?
Create a revision timetable and stick to it, breaking tasks into small steps to make them manageable.
Can listening to music help during revision?
Listening to calm, instrumental music can improve focus, but avoid distracting songs with lyrics.
