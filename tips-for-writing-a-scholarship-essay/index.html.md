# Everything You Need to Know about Writing a Scholarship Essay ...

Since many of the scholarship programs involve writing a scholarship worthy essay and very few programs are based solely on an application form or transcript,tips for writing a scholarship essay can give you an advantage. Even though scholarships are a popular way to finance a college education, it is often quite hard to get into these programs. Most scholarship committees prefer essays because they will get a better sense of who the applicant really is. A winning essay should set a student apart from all the other applicants, so if you tell your story clearly and persuasively, you might convince the donor that you are the right person for their financial support. Here are a few very helpful tips for writing a scholarship essay:

## 1. Analyze the Essay Question

One of the most important tips for writing a scholarship essay that I could give you is to tell you to try to read carefully and to analyze the essay question. Read the instructions and make sure that you understand them before you start writing anything. This is not only about understanding the question; you need to also understand why they are asking those things, and to figure out what they are looking for, so you can answer appropriately.

## 2. Research

Research the foundation or school your are applying to before you start writing the essay, since they have core values that they cherish. If you want to impress them and write an outstanding essay, then try to find some of the key terms and core values that they often use and work those ideas or terms into your essay.

## 3. Answer the Question

A lot of students make interesting points but they are not about the question at hand. Don’t just answer what you think they asked. Answer what they really asked. If you are asked a specific question, then stick to it. Even though it can be quite tempting to list your merits when you are asked about environmental problems, just stick to that topic because otherwise, your answer could really hurt your chances at a scholarship.

## 4. Show That You Are Well Rounded

A lot of amazing and talented students send in scholarship essays every year, so if you want to stand out from the crowd, you need to convince the readers that you are a well-rounded person; basically that you are more than just good grades. List organizations you have worked with and mention hobbies to which you dedicate your time.

## 5. Add a Personal Touch

Don’t forget to add a personal touch to your essay, because even though credentials are what you need to get accepted into a prestigious university, a dash of personality can take your essay above and beyond. Use humor or share an interesting (but relevant to your topic) personal story and your essay will definitely be among the winning ones.

## 6. Use Firm Language

When you write an application essay for a scholarship, you should use firm language and a passionate tone. Make sure your grammar and spelling are impeccable and use clear, simple and concise language throughout the entire essay. Avoid using words or phrases like “maybe”, “sort of”, “I hope” or “I try” and replace them with “I’m sure”, “I will” and “I can”.

## 7. Proofread

Before sending your essay, make sure that you proofread it carefully, so you spot mistakes. Put your paper through a thorough spell and grammar check, so you can make sure that your essay will stand out for its excellence and not for its errors. You can even ask one of your friends or one of your family members with strong writing and editing skills to help you.

Writing an application essay for a scholarship can be a bit difficult but if you keep in mind the tips presented in this article, you will surely manage to reach your goals and obtain that scholarship.
